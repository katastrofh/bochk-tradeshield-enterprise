# Hard-review fixes applied in V4

## Criticism 1: The intuition of the app does not make sense
Fixed by making the Risk Passport the central product object and reorganizing the interface around a banking lifecycle.

## Criticism 2: UI is not understandable
Fixed with a left navigation, step-by-step demo guide, workflow readiness, case queue, risk passport, decision/settlement, fraud network, and governance sections.

## Criticism 3: It looks like a demo
Fixed by adding enterprise controls: role-based access, decision gates, escalation rules, settlement preconditions, audit hash-chain, model registry, PDF risk report.

## Criticism 4: Settlement and ESG feel bolted on
Fixed by embedding both in the case lifecycle. Settlement now requires approval plus supplier and shipment verification. ESG is recorded as green-trade eligibility evidence.

## Criticism 5: Risk score is too black-box
Fixed by adding sub-scores, top risk drivers, mitigating factors, required controls, recommended amount, and narrative.

## Criticism 6: Fraud graph is too abstract
Fixed by linking cases, SMEs, suppliers, bank accounts, buyers, corridors, changed accounts, and shared accounts with a plain-English fraud narrative.

## Criticism 7: Raw JSON makes it look like a developer toy
Fixed by replacing JSON-first views with cards, badges, score bars, tables, timelines, and narrative text.
